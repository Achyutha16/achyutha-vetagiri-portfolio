Project

Healthcare Identity Management

Designing Healthcare Identity Management to secure patient profile information, grant and deny user access rights, and manage better identity governance. It protects sensitive data within the system and validates an individual's role in accessing that data.

My work

As a UI designer for Healthcare Identity Management, I designed the user interface screens based on the requirements gathered from stakeholders.

Here are some of the key screens that I designed:

  • Authentication and authorization Screen: The screens require users to provide secure login credentials, such as a username and password, to access their health information. This helps to ensure that only authorized individuals can view and manage the data.
  • Health data input Screen: Users can input their personal health information into the platform, which include medical history, allergies, medications, test results, and other relevant health data.
  • Health data visualization Screen: The screens may display the health data in an easy-to-understand format, such as charts or graphs, to help individuals better understand their health status and identify trends over time.
  • Health data sharing Screen: Users may have the ability to share their health data with healthcare providers, family members, or other authorized individuals, depending on the platform's privacy and security policies.
  • Security and privacy Screen: The screens include features to ensure the security and privacy of health data, such as encryption, data backup, and user access controls.
  • Notifications and alerts Screen: The screens may provide users with notifications and alerts for important health events, such as medication reminders or appointment scheduling.

User Research

When conducting UX research on Healthcare Identity Management Software, there are several key points of concern that should be considered:

Mobile optimization: Many users access recruitment tools on mobile devices

Competitor analysis: what other recruitment tools are available in the market

Technical constraints:limited functionality or need to integrate with existing systems

Balance competing interests: while still prioritizing the needs of the users

Create user journey maps:to improve the user experience.
