Sketch, Wireframe, Mockup, and Prototype

Sketching: This is the initial stage of the design process where I create rough sketches of your ideas. Sketching is a fast and low-fidelity way to generate and communicate ideas.
Wireframing:After sketching, the next step is to create wireframes. Wireframes are more detailed than sketches and help to define the layout, structure, and functionality of my design. Wireframes can be created on paper or using digital tools.
Mockups: Once I have a wireframe, I can move on to creating a mockup. Mockups are more high-fidelity representations of my design, often including color, typography, and images. I provide a clearer sense of the final product.
Prototyping: The final stage of the design process is prototyping. A prototype is a functional representation of design that users can interact with. Prototyping helps me test design and get feedback from users before the final product is developed.
